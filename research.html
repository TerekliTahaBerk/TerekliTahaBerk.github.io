R / 01 · principal investigator

Brain Tumor Segmentation with U-Net— a TÜBİTAK 2209-A funded undergraduate research project

In progress · 2025

Glioma sub-region segmentation on multi-modal MRI is one of those problems where the last 5% of Dice is where all the engineering lives. The clinically useful part — separating enhancing tumor from necrotic core and surrounding edema — is rarely about a fancier backbone. It is about boring things: skull-stripping consistency, intensity normalisation, patch sampling, and not lying to yourself with the validation split.

This project takes the original U-Net as a baseline and rebuilds it carefully in TensorFlow / Keras, then iterates on three axes: data (BraTS-style 4-channel volumes, on-the-fly augmentation, class-balanced patch sampling), loss (Dice + categorical cross-entropy, then a focal Tversky variant for the small enhancing class), and evaluation (per-class Dice, IoU, HD95, and an honest per-patient breakdown rather than dataset-averaged numbers).

The deeper question I keep coming back to is not "can it segment?" — modern U-Nets can — but "can a junior researcher reproduce this pipeline end-to-end on a single GPU in a week?" The artifact I want to leave behind is a clean, documented, single-author baseline that other students at my university can fork without inheriting a thousand-line undocumented codebase.

Method, in four panels

01 · DATA
Curate & normalise

4-channel MRI volumes (T1, T1ce, T2, FLAIR), per-volume z-score normalisation, skull-strip sanity checks, and a patient-disjoint train/val/test split.

02 · MODEL
U-Net, opinionated

Classical contracting / expanding path with instance norm + GELU, group convolutions in the bottleneck, deep supervision at two scales.

03 · LOSS
Dice + CE, then Tversky

Soft Dice combined with categorical cross-entropy as the workhorse; focal Tversky as an ablation for the under-represented enhancing-tumor class.

04 · EVAL
Honest metrics

Per-class Dice / IoU / HD95 reported as median + IQR across patients — not a single inflated mean — and qualitative slice galleries for every failure case.
